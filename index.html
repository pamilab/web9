About The System

Ground-penetrating-radar systems are widely used in civil engineering, subsurface utility mapping, archaeology, and safety inspections to locate subsurface objects without digging. However, many commercial GPR devices are expensive, bulky, and consume more power, limiting their use in academia, small entities, and research applications.

To mitigate these constraints, the present work describes the development of a low-cost portable FMCW GPR prototype assembled from commercial off-the-shelf RF modules, open software tools, and a motorized scanning platform. The system operates within the 2.4 GHz ISM band for reasons of component availability and suitability for shallow subsurface detection.

This system adopts FMCW technology that allows for stable signal transmission and increased noise tolerance compared to impulse-based radar architecture. Reflected signals will be processed using STFT techniques along with SDR digitization to clearly visualize subsurface objects and estimate depth.
