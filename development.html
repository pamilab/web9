Building the Prototype

From concept and simulation to hardware integration and validation.

1. Concept and Planning

Identifying system limitations and defining FMCW radar requirements.

System constraints such as operating frequency, antenna size, regulatory compliance, and cost were evaluated. FMCW radar was selected for its range resolution and SDR compatibility.

2. Design and Simulation

Modeling chirp behavior and depth–frequency relationships.

MATLAB and Python simulations validated beat frequency behavior, maximum depth, and expected range resolution prior to hardware build.

3. Hardware Integration

Assembly of RF modules and SDR hardware.

HackRF SDR, antennas, mixers, and power modules were integrated into a mechanically stable platform to reduce signal drift.

4. Software Development

Signal acquisition, STFT processing, and visualization.

GNU Radio and Python scripts handled real-time digitization, STFT processing, and radargram generation.

5. Testing & Validation

Controlled subsurface experiments with known target depths.

Metallic targets were embedded at 29 cm and 72 cm depths and compared against manual tape measurements to validate accuracy.
