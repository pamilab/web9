Building the Prototype

From concept and simulation to hardware integration and validation.

1. Concept and Planning

Identifying system limitations and defining FMCW radar requirements.

During this phase, system constraints such as cost, operating frequency, antenna size, and regulatory compliance were evaluated. FMCW radar was selected for its high range resolution and compatibility with low-cost SDR platforms.

2. Design and Simulation

Modeling chirp behavior and depth-frequency relationships.

MATLAB and Python simulations were used to validate beat frequency behavior, maximum detectable depth, and expected resolution prior to hardware deployment.

3. Hardware Integration

Assembly of RF and SDR components.

HackRF SDR, antennas, mixers, and power modules were integrated into a mechanically stable platform to minimize signal drift and environmental noise.

4. Software Development

Signal acquisition and processing implementation.

GNU Radio flowgraphs and Python scripts handled real-time digitization, STFT processing, and radargram visualization.

5. Testing & Validation

Controlled subsurface target testing.

Metallic targets were embedded at known depths (29 cm and 72 cm) to validate depth estimation accuracy against manual measurements.
