Problem Context & Methodology

Bridging the gap between high-cost commercial radar and accessible research tools.

The Problem We Address

Subsurface inspection is critical in civil engineering, disaster response, and underground mapping. However, existing GPR systems are often expensive, bulky, and difficult to customize. These limitations restrict their use to large organizations and specialized operators.

This project aims to bridge the gap by developing a radar system that is:

  • Affordable for institutions and researchers
  • Simple to deploy and operate
  • Reliable in controlled and semi-controlled environments

Our Engineering Approach

The FMCW-GPR prototype was developed using a structured, stage-by-stage engineering methodology. Each phase was validated before progressing to the next, ensuring performance consistency and minimizing integration issues.

How the System Works

1

Transmission

A frequency-swept FMCW chirp is transmitted toward the ground.

2

Reflection

Reflected signals from subsurface objects return to the receiver.

3

Mixing

The transmitted and received signals are mixed, producing a beat frequency.

4

Digitization

Beat frequencies are digitized using an SDR platform.

5

STFT Processing

STFT processing converts the signal into depth-related frequency components.

6

Visualization

Results are visualized as radargrams showing depth and lateral position.
