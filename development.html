1. Concept and Planning

The project began with identifying system limitations in existing GPR designs and selecting FMCW radar as the optimal solution.

Parameter Specification Design Rationale
Radar Type FMCW (Frequency-Modulated Continuous Wave) Enables high range resolution with lower peak power compared to pulsed GPR systems.
Operating Frequency 2.4 GHz (ISM Band) Selected to balance penetration depth and resolution while ensuring regulatory compliance and component availability.
Bandwidth Hundreds of MHz (chirp-dependent) Larger bandwidth improves depth resolution in subsurface target detection.
Target Depth Range Up to ~1 meter (controlled environment) Suitable for laboratory-scale validation and shallow subsurface inspection applications.
Digitization Platform Software-Defined Radio (SDR) Provides flexibility in signal acquisition, processing, and rapid prototyping.
Signal Processing STFT-based analysis Enables time-frequency representation for depth estimation and radargram visualization.
Cost Objective Low-cost research prototype Designed to be accessible for academic and institutional use compared to commercial GPR systems.

2. Design and Simulation

Radar equations, chirp characteristics, and depth-to-frequency relationships were analyzed to predict system performance.

3. Hardware Integration

RF modules, control units, and digitization hardware were assembled into a unified radar front-end.

4. Software Development

Signal acquisition and processing were implemented using GNU Radio and Python.
